## Coaching Philosophy & Communication

### Philosophy: "Technique â†’ Confidence â†’ Speed"

Speed is the *result* of good technique and smart training, not the goal of every practice. We build the foundation first, and the speed follows. For kids who've never raced, **confidence is the most important thing we can give them before May 2nd.**

### Key Messages for Athletes
> 1. **"We're building rowers, not just racing."** Every practice makes you better. Trust the process.
> 2. **"Technique is your superpower."** The crew that rows together, cleanly, beats the crew with one fast kid rowing ugly.
> 3. **"Hard work is a team sport."** When it hurts in a race, your crewmates are hurting too. You don't quit on each other.
> 4. **"Your first race will be scary and awesome."** Everyone's nervous. That's normal. We'll be ready.

### Key Messages for Parents
> 1. **Progress over results.** Their son's first racing season is about learning, not winning. Improvement is the metric.
> 2. **Consistency matters most.** Missing practice hurts the whole crew. Please prioritize attendance.
> 3. **Nutrition & sleep are training.** 8+ hours of sleep, good breakfast, water bottle every practice. No energy drinks.
> 4. **Let the coach coach.** After a race, ask "Did you have fun? What did you learn?" not "Why didn't you win?"
> 5. **Injury communication is critical.** If your son mentions pain (especially back, ribs, knees), tell us immediately. We'd rather modify training for a week than lose him for a month.

## Boat Selection Philosophy

### Guiding Principles
1. **The fastest boat is the one that rows best together** â€” not necessarily the 4 fastest erg scores in a quad.
2. **Timing and blade work trump raw power** at the novice level. A technically clean crew will beat a strong but sloppy crew.
3. **Everyone races.** Every kid who commits to the season gets a seat. We may have A and B boats, but both race.
4. **Seat racing is fair and transparent.** When we do selection pieces, everyone knows what we're measuring and why.

### Selection Process (Timeline)
| When | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Weeks 1â€“3 | Rotate everyone through all seats/boats | See who moves well, who pairs well, who leads |
| Week 4 | First erg benchmark (NOT a 2k â€” see below) | Baseline fitness data |
| Weeks 4â€“6 | Seat racing pieces (short on-water or erg) | Head-to-head comparison in context |
| Week 6 | Preliminary lineups announced | Give crews time to gel (6 weeks to States) |
| Weeks 7â€“9 | Crew-specific training, minor adjustments only | Build crew chemistry and confidence |
| Week 10 | Final lineup confirmed | No changes unless injury |

### Erg Testing for 8th Graders
**DO NOT start with a 2k test.** These kids aren't ready for that physically or mentally.

| Week | Test | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Week 4 | **4 Ã— 500m** (2 min rest) | Short enough to not be terrifying, reveals fitness + pacing ability |
| Week 7 | **2 Ã— 1000m** (3 min rest) | Builds toward race distance, teaches pacing a longer piece |
| Week 9 | **1500m time trial** (race distance) | Full race simulation on erg, confidence builder |

### Boat Types (Adjust to Your Fleet)
For novice 8th grade boys, prioritize stability:
- **Ideal starting boat**: 4x+ (coxed quad) or 8+ â€” most stable, coach can communicate via cox
- **As skills develop**: 4x (quad without cox), 2x (double), possibly 1x for experienced fall rowers
- **Race entries for States**: Enter the most stable, practiced lineup. If you have enough for an 8+, that's often the best novice boat (stable, team experience, exciting).
