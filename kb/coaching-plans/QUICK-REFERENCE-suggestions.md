### 1. Add Explicit Warm-Up Protocol (Appendix)
**Why**: Consistency + injury prevention  
**What**: 10-min sequence including hip mobility, thoracic rotation, scapular activation, hamstring prep, core activation  
**When**: Use before every practice  

### 2. Create Race Day Timeline (Email #4 or Appendix)
**Why**: Reduces chaos and anxiety for first-time racers  
**What**: Minute-by-minute schedule from arrival (90 min before) to post-race  
**Example**: 75 min = change into unis, 60 min = launch, 45 min = drills, 30 min = paddle to staging, etc.

### 3. Add Injury Red Flags Education (Week 1 + Parent Email #1)
**Why**: 13-14 year olds at high risk for growth plate injuries  
**What**: Teach difference between RED flags (sharp pain, rib/back pain that persists) vs. GREEN (muscle soreness)  
**Include**: "Tell coach immediately" protocol

### 4. Implement Technical Assessment Rubric (Weeks 2, 6, 9)
**Why**: Makes "technique over speed" measurable and motivating  
**What**: Rate 1-5 on: catch position, drive sequence, extraction, recovery, overall rhythm  
**Share**: Individual progress with each athlete

### 5. Add Video Analysis Sessions
- **Week 2**: Record ergs (side view), show athletes their stroke
- **Week 4**: Record on-water, team review
- **Week 7**: Record race sim, compare to Week 4
- **Why**: Athletes learn faster when they can SEE their technique

### 6. Emphasize Ratio Work (Weeks 2-4)
- Make 2:1 recovery ratio a weekly focus
- Add drills: pause at hands-away for 2 counts, count to 3 on recovery
- **Why**: Prevents rushing, the #1 novice fault

### 7. Create Race Nutrition Guide (Email #4)
- Night before: pasta/rice dinner, hydrate, early bed
- Morning of: breakfast 2-3 hours before (oatmeal + banana)
- Avoid: heavy/fatty foods, dairy, anything new, energy drinks
- After: chocolate milk + snack within 30 min

### 8. Adjust Taper Timing
- **Current**: 2 weeks (Weeks 10-11)
- **Better for novices**: 1.5 weeks
  - Week 10: Moderate cut (20-30% volume)
  - Week 11: Sharp cut (50% volume, last hard work Tuesday)
- **Why**: Novices don't accumulate as much fatigue; too long = detraining
